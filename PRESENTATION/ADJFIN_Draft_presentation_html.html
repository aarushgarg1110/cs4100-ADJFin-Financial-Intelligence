Financial Intelligence with Deep RL

CS 4100 Project

Research Question: Do reinforcement learning agents learn superior long-term financial strategies compared to rule-based human approaches?

Environment: 30-year lifecycle simulation with stochastic market regimes, life events, and multi-dimensional state/action spaces

Methodology: Deep Q-Network training with experience replay, evaluated against Age-Based, Markowitz, 60/40, Equal Weight, and Debt Avalanche baselines

Reward Engineering - The Sharpe Ratio

How Do We Teach Risk Management?

Hybrid Reward Function

reward = (1 - w) × wealth + w × sharpe_ratio

Wealth Component

Encourages getting rich

wealth_reward = net_worth / $1M

$1M = 1.0 reward, $2M = 2.0 reward

Sharpe Ratio Component

Encourages smart risk-taking

sharpe = (return - risk_free) / volatility

Higher return per unit of risk

What We Learned

1. RL Has Potential

Can match or exceed human performance ($2.22M vs $2.14M)

Learns complex strategies from experience without explicit programming

2. Reward Engineering Matters

  • • 60% Sharpe weight → aggressive, high returns
  • • 30% Sharpe weight → balanced, appropriate behavior
  • • Wrong reward = dangerous agent despite high performance

3. Evaluation Must Go Beyond Returns

Traditional metrics (net worth) are insufficient

Need behavioral analysis:

  • • Q-policy testing (age/market appropriateness)
  • • Action diversity
  • • Safety considerations

4. The RL Advantage

Unlike rule-based strategies, RL can learn nuanced policies that adapt to:

  • • Current debt levels
  • • Life events (job loss, medical emergencies)
  • • Market conditions
  • • Personal financial state

The Path Forward

Future Directions

1. Age-Aware Rewards

Explicitly penalize age-inappropriate risk

penalty = max(0, stock_pct - (100 - age))

2. Constrained RL

Hard limits on allocation

• stocks ≤ (100 - age)%

• emergency_fund ≥ 6 months expenses

3. Multi-Objective Optimization

Optimize Pareto frontier of returns vs safety

Find policies that balance wealth and risk optimally

4. Compare PPO

Test policy gradient methods

May learn smoother, more stable policies than DQN

Conclusion

RL can learn competitive financial strategies, but naive reward functions produce reckless behavior. Success requires careful reward engineering and multi-dimensional evaluation. Our work demonstrates both the promise and pitfalls of RL in personal finance.
